Focus Areas for Kindergarten

  • Understanding numbers, names, and the count sequence
  • Counting to tell the number of objects
  • Understanding addition as putting together and subtraction as taking apart
  • Working with numbers 11-19 to gain foundations for place value
  • Describing and comparing measurable attributes

Math Standards by Domain

Counting and Cardinality (K.CC)

Know Number Names and Count Sequence

K.CC.1-3 Count to 100 by ones and tens, count forward from any number, write numbers 0-20.

Count to Tell Number of Objects

K.CC.4-5 Understand relationship between numbers and quantities, count to answer "how many?"

Compare Numbers

K.CC.6-7 Identify whether the number of objects in one group is greater than, less than, or equal to another group.

Operations and Algebraic Thinking (K.OA)

Understand Addition and Subtraction

K.OA.1-2 Represent addition and subtraction with objects, fingers, mental images, drawings, sounds, acting out situations, verbal explanations, expressions, or equations.

Work with Numbers to Gain Foundations

K.OA.3-5 Decompose numbers less than or equal to 10, fluently add and subtract within 5.

Number and Operations in Base Ten (K.NBT)

Work with Numbers 11-19

K.NBT.1 Compose and decompose numbers 11-19 into ten ones and some further ones.

Measurement and Data (K.MD)

Describe and Compare Measurable Attributes

K.MD.1-2 Describe measurable attributes of objects, compare two objects with a measurable attribute in common.

Classify Objects and Count

K.MD.3 Classify objects into given categories; count the numbers of objects in each category.

Geometry (K.G)

Identify and Describe Shapes

K.G.1-3 Describe objects in the environment using names of shapes, correctly name shapes.

Analyze, Compare, and Create Shapes

K.G.4-6 Analyze and compare shapes, model shapes by building and drawing, compose simple shapes to form larger shapes.
