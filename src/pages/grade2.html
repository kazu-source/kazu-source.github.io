Focus Areas for Second Grade

  • Extending understanding of base-ten notation
  • Building fluency with addition and subtraction
  • Using standard units of measure
  • Describing and analyzing shapes

Math Standards by Domain

Operations and Algebraic Thinking (2.OA)

Represent and Solve Problems

2.OA.1 Use addition and subtraction within 100 to solve one- and two-step word problems involving situations of adding to, taking from, putting together, taking apart, and comparing.

Add and Subtract Within 20

2.OA.2 Fluently add and subtract within 20 using mental strategies. By end of Grade 2, know from memory all sums of two one-digit numbers.

Work with Equal Groups

2.OA.3 Determine whether a group of objects (up to 20) has an odd or even number of members.

2.OA.4 Use addition to find the total number of objects arranged in rectangular arrays with up to 5 rows and up to 5 columns.

Number and Operations in Base Ten (2.NBT)

Understand Place Value

2.NBT.1 Understand that the three digits of a three-digit number represent amounts of hundreds, tens, and ones.

2.NBT.2 Count within 1000; skip-count by 5s, 10s, and 100s.

2.NBT.3 Read and write numbers to 1000 using base-ten numerals, number names, and expanded form.

2.NBT.4 Compare two three-digit numbers using >, =, and < symbols.

Use Place Value Understanding and Properties of Operations

2.NBT.5 Fluently add and subtract within 100.

2.NBT.6 Add up to four two-digit numbers.

2.NBT.7 Add and subtract within 1000.

2.NBT.9 Explain why addition and subtraction strategies work.

Measurement and Data (2.MD)

Measure and Estimate Lengths

2.MD.1-4 Measure and estimate lengths in standard units.

Relate Addition and Subtraction to Length

2.MD.5-6 Use addition and subtraction within 100 to solve word problems involving lengths.

Work with Time and Money

2.MD.7-8 Tell and write time from analog and digital clocks. Solve word problems involving dollar bills and coins.

Represent and Interpret Data

2.MD.9-10 Generate measurement data and show it in a line plot. Draw a picture graph and a bar graph to represent data.

Geometry (2.G)

Reason with Shapes and Their Attributes

2.G.1 Recognize and draw shapes having specified attributes.

2.G.2 Partition a rectangle into rows and columns of same-size squares.

2.G.3 Partition circles and rectangles into two, three, or four equal shares.
